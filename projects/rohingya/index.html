AI in humanitarian context

More than 723000 Rohingya people from the Rakhine state of Myanmar entered in Bangladesh in the year 2017. Now the number of refugees enter in Bangladesh has crossed more than 1.1 million. This colossal number of people are living in a dense condition with a high risk of landslide, cyclone, flash flood, and infectious disease. Our aim is to develop a machine learning-based early warning system for this natural disaster to save the lives and wealth of this huge number of refugees.

Improving spatial agreement in machine learning-based landslide susceptibility mapping
  • Around 1.1 million Rohingya refugees are living in Cox's Bazar region of Bangladesh with high landslide risk. To help these refugee people, we combine machine learning and remote sensing (satellite/GIS data) to predict landslide and developing landslide susceptibility maps, which will help these 1.1 million refugees to save their lives and wealth. Our machine learning algorithm predicts landslide with 99% accuracy.
