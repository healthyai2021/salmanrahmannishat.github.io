I am a PhD student in the Machine Learning Department at Carnegie Mellon University, co-advised by Tom Mitchell and Barnabàs Pòczos.

My research focuses on artificial intelligence applications in sustainability problems, more specifically computational sustainability. I am broadly working on machine learning to tackle future climate, agriculture and energy challenges. I combine machine learning and remote sensing (satellite data) to tackle the sustainability challenges. Besides, I am interested in markov decision process,network algorithm, dynamic programming reinforcement learning and game theory.

Before I joined CMU, I graduated with an M.Phil. in Advanced Computer Science from the University of Cambridge, UK. In my Master's thesis I used Machine Learning methods to detect and align chromosomes in microscope images, advised by Pietro Lió.
